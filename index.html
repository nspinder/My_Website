What Is AML?

Anti-money laundering (AML) comprises laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. Financial institutions apply Know Your Customer (KYC) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD) processes to establish identity and risk, then use transaction monitoring and alerting to identify suspicious activity across the stages of placement, layering, and integration.

Why Are AML Regulations Important

AML regulations prevent illicit funds from flowing through the financial system, reducing fraud, corruption, and terrorism financing. Strong compliance programs protect institutions from fines and reputational harm while supporting legitimate business growth. Effective programs include risk-based monitoring, evidence of training, and governance that demonstrate proactive management to regulators.
